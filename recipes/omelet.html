Omelet

Description

An omelet, or omelette, is a deceptively simple yet versatile dish made from beaten eggs quickly cooked in a skillet until just firm, often folded around a filling such as cheese, vegetables, mushrooms, meats, or a combination of these. Originating from French cuisine, the omelet has become a worldwide breakfast staple, appreciated for its light and fluffy texture, and its ability to be tailored to individual tastes. The key to a perfect omelet lies in its preparation â€” the eggs are whisked to incorporate air, ensuring a fluffy result, and then cooked in a well-buttered or oiled pan over medium heat. As the eggs begin to set, they're gently lifted at the edges to allow uncooked egg to flow underneath. Once the base is set but the top is slightly runny, fillings are added, and the omelet is expertly folded, resulting in a semi-circle or roll shape. Quick to prepare, omelets are not only a breakfast favorite but also a go-to meal for any time of the day, offering endless possibilities for customization.

Ingredients

  • Eggs - 2 or 3
  • Salt - a pinch
  • Black pepper - to taste
  • Butter or oil - for cooking
  • Optional fillings: cheese, diced vegetables, cooked meats, herbs

Steps

  1. Beat the eggs in a bowl, add salt and pepper to taste.
  2. Heat butter or oil in a non-stick skillet over medium heat.
  3. Pour the beaten eggs into the skillet, tilting the pan to spread them evenly.
  4. Cook until the edges start to lift from the pan. Use a spatula to gently lift the edges and allow uncooked eggs to flow beneath.
  5. When the top is almost set, add your choice of fillings on one half of the omelet.
  6. Carefully fold the other half of the omelet over the filling.
  7. Cook for another minute or until the omelet is cooked to your liking.
  8. Gently slide the omelet onto a plate and serve immediately.
