Lasagna

Description

Lasagna, a beloved dish of Italian origin, is a culinary masterpiece that harmonizes layers of tender pasta with rich, savory fillings. Each layer is typically comprised of wide, flat noodles, alternated with generous portions of seasoned ground meat—often a mix of beef and pork—and sometimes interspersed with slices of vegetables like zucchini or spinach. The layers are luxuriously smothered in a robust tomato sauce, infused with herbs such as basil and oregano, which imparts a tangy yet sweet flavor. Integral to its richness is the creamy béchamel or ricotta cheese mixture, which melds seamlessly with the other components upon baking. The crown of this gastronomic jewel is a crusty, golden-brown top, formed by a final layer of grated mozzarella or Parmesan cheese that becomes bubbly and slightly crisp in the oven. This hearty, comforting dish is a staple at family gatherings and a classic choice in Italian restaurants, celebrated for its delightful blend of textures and depth of flavor.

Ingredients

  • Olive oil - 2 tablespoons
  • Ground beef - 1 pound
  • Ground pork or Italian sausage - 1/2 pound (optional)
  • Onion, finely chopped - 1 medium
  • Garlic cloves, minced - 2-3
  • Canned crushed tomatoes - 28 ounces
  • Tomato paste - 2 tablespoons
  • Fresh basil, chopped - 2 tablespoons

Steps

  1. Preheat oven to 375°F (190°C).
  2. In a large skillet, heat olive oil over medium heat. Add the ground beef and pork, cook until browned.
  3. Add chopped onions and minced garlic to the skillet. Cook until the onions are translucent.
  4. Stir in crushed tomatoes, tomato paste, basil, oregano, and a pinch of salt and pepper. Simmer the sauce for about 15-20 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  5. In a separate bowl, mix ricotta cheese with egg, a portion of the grated Parmesan, and a bit of salt and pepper.
  6. To assemble the lasagna, spread a layer of meat sauce in the bottom of a baking dish. Place lasagna noodles over the sauce.
  7. Spread a layer of the ricotta mixture over the noodles, then add a layer of shredded mozzarella.
  8. Repeat the layers (sauce, noodles, ricotta mixture, mozzarella) until all ingredients are used, finishing with a layer of sauce and mozzarella on top.
  9. Sprinkle the remaining Parmesan cheese over the top layer.
  10. Cover the dish with foil and bake for 25 minutes. Then remove the foil and bake for another 25 minutes, or until the cheese is bubbly and golden brown.
  11. Let the lasagna cool for 10-15 minutes before serving.
